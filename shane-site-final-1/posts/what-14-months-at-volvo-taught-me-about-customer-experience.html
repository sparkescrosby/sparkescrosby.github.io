When I started my internship at Volvo Car Ireland, I expected “customer experience” to mean nice interiors, good coffee, and tidy showrooms.

It’s bigger than that. CX is the cumulative result of hundreds of micro-decisions, across marketing, retail, digital, and service.

1. The handover is the product

For a lot of customers, the handover is the moment they decide whether the brand is truly premium. Clear communication, calm pacing, and small details matter.

2. Consistency beats intensity

A single “wow” moment can’t compensate for small friction across the journey. The best CX is often invisible.

3. Make it easy to say yes

Good CX removes uncertainty. It answers questions before they are asked, and it reduces the cognitive load at decision time.

One line: Great CX is not a department, it is a standard.
